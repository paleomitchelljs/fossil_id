Outline & proportion

Max half-width vs length. High = transverse (spiriferid).
0 = pointed beak (non-strophic); 1 = wide straight hinge (strophic).
0 = widest at hinge (spiriferid/strophomenid); ~0.5 = mid (atrypid).
Width of the front margin: low = tapered/pointed, high = broadly rounded.

Convexity (inflation)

Equal = biconvex; near 0 = plano-convex; negative = concavo-convex (strophomenid).
Incurves and raises the ventral umbo over the hinge.

Commissure (fold & sulcus)

1 = uniplicate (single fold); higher = multicostate / plicate.

Cardinal area

Opening in the interarea below the beak. Most visible on wide-hinged, big-beaked forms.

Ornament (radial costae)

0 = smooth; few&coarse = spiriferid plicae; many&fine = costellae.
Relief of the ribs. Fades at the margins so the valves still close.
Comarginal lamellae. 0 = none; few&deep = imbricate, many&fine = striate.
Spine bases scattered over the surface (e.g. Spinatrypa).
